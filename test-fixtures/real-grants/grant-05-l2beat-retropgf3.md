# L2BEAT — Independent L2 Transparency Watchdog

**Grant Program**: Optimism RetroPGF Round 3 (announced January 2024)
**Amount**: 256,000 OP (received, retroactive)
**Source URL**: https://optimism.mirror.xyz/37Bgum6MfTJWDuE41CH9RXSH5KBm_RCL5zsSFeRZl4E

## Project Description

L2BEAT is an independent, impartial watchdog for Ethereum Layer 2s. It publishes standardized comparisons of L2 security models, stages, TVL, activity, upgrade keys, and risk parameters at l2beat.com. The site has become the de-facto reference for L2 security assessments used by users, developers, auditors, and researchers across the ecosystem. RetroPGF Round 3 rewards past contributions rather than funding future work, so the "proposal" is really an impact statement describing public goods already delivered to the Optimism Collective and the broader L2 ecosystem.

## Team

L2BEAT is led by Bartek Kiepuszewski and Piotr Szlachciak with a small full-time research and engineering team. The team is fiercely independent — it publicly declines product sponsorships from L2s and structures itself to avoid conflicts of interest. Its mission statement: "to be an independent and impartial watchdog that provides on-chain transparency."

## Milestones & Deliverables (past contributions considered)

- L2 Stages framework (Stage 0 / 1 / 2) — now an industry-standard maturity model
- Public L2 risk dashboards covering all major Optimism Superchain and non-OP L2s
- DA Layer tracking post-Dencun
- Open-source methodology and data repositories on GitHub
- Research publications on upgrade keys, sequencer decentralization, and withdrawal guarantees

## Budget Breakdown (use of retroactive funding)

L2BEAT stated that the 256,000 OP will fund continued public-goods production consistent with its mission — no token incentives to users, no marketing spend. The funds sustain research staff, data pipelines, and site hosting.

## Impact Claims

- Makes the L2 ecosystem more transparent and safer by surfacing upgrade keys, security assumptions, and risk parameters that individual L2s would not proactively disclose
- Creates pressure on L2s to improve security and decentralization to earn higher stages
- Used widely by Optimism governance, auditors, journalists, and protocols deciding where to deploy
- Directly aligned with the Optimism Collective's values around transparency and credible neutrality

## Outcome (if known)

Awarded 256,000 OP in RetroPGF Round 3 as one of 501 recipients sharing a 30M OP pool. L2BEAT publicly reflected that the award validated their pure-public-goods model and encouraged them to continue resisting commercial pressures. A strong test case for judges on a non-DeFi, non-TVL public good where "impact" is measured in transparency and industry-standard setting rather than dollar volume.
