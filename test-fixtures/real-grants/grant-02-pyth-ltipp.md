# Pyth Network — Gas Rebate Program on Arbitrum

**Grant Program**: Arbitrum Long Term Incentives Pilot Program (LTIPP), March 2024
**Amount**: 1,000,000 ARB requested (received; only ~66,300 ARB actually distributed after Dencun)
**Source URL**: https://forum.arbitrum.foundation/t/pyth-network-ltipp-application-draft/22003

## Project Description

Pyth Network is a first-party financial oracle that streams low-latency market data to smart contracts across 50+ blockchain ecosystems. It publishes 400+ real-time price feeds spanning crypto, equities, ETFs, FX pairs, and commodities, and has facilitated over $100B in cumulative on-chain trading volume. This proposal funds a gas-fee rebate program that fully reimburses Arbitrum applications for the gas cost of pulling Pyth price updates, removing the primary cost friction to consuming the oracle.

## Team

Two primary contributors: the Pyth Data Association (ecosystem growth and partnerships) and Douro Labs (core engineering). Named contributors include Marc Tillement (applicant), Pierre, Ed, Mario, and Jayant. Pyth is one of the most widely integrated oracles in web3 with deep institutional data-provider relationships (Jane Street, Binance, CBOE, etc.).

## Milestones & Deliverables

- **Month 1–2**: Deploy rebate-claim mechanism; advertise to Arbitrum builders
- **Month 2–3**: Ramp-up — onboard 20 new price feeds, reimburse 100% of gas, attract 10,000 new end users
- Weekly activity-based disbursement of rebates
- Public dashboards for update counts, rebate totals, and feed coverage

## Budget Breakdown

- **1,000,000 ARB** — Gas-fee update rebates, disbursed weekly based on verified on-chain update activity. Sized from a target of 25,000 price updates per day × ~$1 per update × 12 weeks.

Entire allocation routed to rebates; no team expenses funded.

## Impact Claims

By year-end 2024, relative to pre-grant baseline:
- Daily consuming smart contracts: 230 → 750
- End users: 7,750 → 40,000
- Daily price updates: 3,500 → 25,000
- Total value secured: $175M → $500M
- Enable $50B in Arbitrum trading volume

## Outcome (if known)

Approved and deployed. The post-Dencun blob upgrade on Ethereum L1 dramatically reduced Arbitrum L2 data-availability costs; average Pyth update costs fell from roughly $5 to $0.03. As a result, only ~66,300 ARB of the 1,000,000 ARB budget was distributed over 11 weeks — a striking demonstration that the budget request was an order of magnitude larger than actually needed, and a useful test case for the Cost Efficiency judge.
