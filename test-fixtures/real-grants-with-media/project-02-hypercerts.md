# Hypercerts

**Grant program**: Protocol Labs (incubation), Gitcoin GG20 Hypercerts Ecosystem Round, Octant Epoch grantee
**Amount**: $60,000+ in matching funds during GG20 Hypercerts Ecosystem Round (across 35 projects), ~$35k in crowdfunded contributions to Hypercerts Foundation, additional Protocol Labs + Octant allocations
**Status/outcome**: Mixed. The original monorepo at `hypercerts-org/hypercerts` was archived on 2025-02-05 and split into separate repositories (protocol, frontend, SDK, docs). The project pivoted toward a smaller scoped "interoperable data layer" framing; adoption remained narrower than early vision promised.

## Project summary
Hypercerts are a semi-fungible ERC-1155 token standard for representing "claims of impact" — a fraction of a hypercert represents a fractional claim on the credit for a specific piece of work in a specific time window and scope. The goal is to make retrospective public-goods funding composable: any funder (Gitcoin round, grant DAO, retroactive reward program) can cite and reward the same underlying impact unit.

The problem Hypercerts targets is fragmentation in impact funding — each program has its own proposal format, scoring rubric, and settlement layer, so contributions cannot accumulate reputation across programs. Hypercerts attempts a shared primitive so that one project can hold claims minted by many funders.

Intellectually influential (widely cited in the public-goods funding literature, co-authored with Protocol Labs, Vitalik, and EA Forum contributors), but real-world adoption has been thin: few funders adopted it as their native settlement layer, and the main repo archive in early 2025 signals a scope reset rather than a successful graduation.

## Team
Originated at Protocol Labs with contributors from the EA/impact-markets community. Hypercerts Foundation spun out to steward the standard. Team has strong research pedigree but the project has been through multiple scope pivots.

## Budget
GG20 Hypercerts Ecosystem Round matched ~$60k across 35 ecosystem projects (avg ~$1.7k each). The foundation itself has received multi-six-figure allocations from Protocol Labs and Octant for core protocol + SDK development.

## Why this is a good judge-agent test case
Borderline case — strong on Impact Potential (visionary primitive, high-prestige backers) but weak signals on Technical Feasibility (archived monorepo, fragmented repos) and arguably Cost Efficiency (sustained funding, limited adoption to date). Good stress test for whether judges pick up on pivot/archive signals in the GitHub README versus being swayed by brand-name funders in the proposal text.
