## What It Is

Ipê City is a **startup society** initiative building digital-native alternatives to traditional nation-state governance. The core thesis: traditional governance runs on a "250-year-old operating system" based on political institutions secured by guns, while modern economies run on digital-native systems using internet and blockchains, based on networks and open-source code, secured by cryptography.

Ipê City aims to prototype a future where democracy and governance rest on open, decentralized technologies, built by agile, startup-like institutions.

Positioned within **network state** theory (Balaji Srinivasan): "a highly aligned online community with a capacity for collective action that crowdfunds territory around the world and eventually gains diplomatic recognition from pre-existing states."

## PULSE System — Collective Action Framework

### Definition
Coordinated simultaneous actions by community members. "One of the key differences is regarding collective action, which we call Pulse around here."

### Operations
- **Frequency:** Twice monthly (2nd and 16th)
- **Duration:** 24-hour execution window per PULSE
- **Initial Format:** Twitter/X engagement (liking, sharing aligned content)
- **Evolution:** Tasks increase in complexity as execution capacity grows

### Governance
- Community members submit content proposals via Discord's Pulse channel
- Community votes to approve and prioritize
- Submission eligibility: participation in all previous 10 PULSEs
- 100% adherence expected
- Non-compliance = permanent record notation; extreme failures → membership revocation

### Future
- Evolving toward "a decentralized protocol and open standard"
- Dashboards monitoring organization health: census data, engagement metrics, token metrics
